evaluation

Students who wish to receive credit for this course are required to write a short essay (or "squib") at the conclusion of this project. The essay should have between 2000 and 3500 words.

The topic of the essay must be "formal pragmatics", broadly construed. Possible topics include, for instance, a description of a novel problem (accompanied by a formal demonstration that it is a problem), a reasonably well executed attempt to fix a known problem, or a comparison of two formal systems. For an example of the latter task, see Hunter & Abrusan (2017).

The essays will be graded on Style, Correctness and Difficulty of the Task. A good essay should make a novel observation about or contribution to the field (even if it is very minor) and demonstrate its consequences in a clear manner. Students are expected to do some amount of independent literature research, in particular where claims of novelty are concerned.
